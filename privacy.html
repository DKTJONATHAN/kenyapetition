Privacy Policy

Compliance with Kenyan Law

This policy complies with the Data Protection Act, 2019 and regulations set by the Office of the Data Protection Commissioner (ODPC) of Kenya.

We adhere to the principles of lawful processing, purpose limitation, data minimization, accuracy, storage limitation, integrity, and confidentiality.

1. Data Collection

In accordance with Section 25 of the Data Protection Act:
We collect only necessary personal data including:

  • Full legal name (for petition verification)
  • County of residence (for geographical representation)
  • Declaration statement (your petition position)
  • Signature initials (as digital consent)
  • IP address (for security purposes only)

We do not collect sensitive personal data as defined under Section 2 of the Act (biometrics, health data, etc.).

2. Lawful Basis & Purpose

Under Article 31(c) of the Constitution of Kenya and Section 30 of the Data Protection Act:
Your data is processed based on explicit consent and for these specific lawful purposes:

  • To authenticate petition signatures
  • To submit verified petitions to relevant authorities
  • To maintain transparency in civic participation
  • To prevent fraudulent submissions

3. Data Retention & Deletion

In compliance with Section 27 (Storage Limitation Principle):
We retain your data only for the period necessary to achieve the petition's purpose:

  • Active petitions: Retained for 90 days after submission to authorities
  • Completed petitions: Anonymized after 180 days
  • All personal data is permanently deleted after 1 year

Exception: Data required by legal process will be retained as mandated by Kenyan courts.

4. Your Rights Under Kenyan Law

As provided in Section 26 of the Data Protection Act, you have:

Right to Access: Request copies of your personal data
Right to Rectification: Correct inaccurate data
Right to Erasure: Request deletion where applicable
Right to Restrict Processing: Limit how we use your data
Right to Data Portability: Receive your data in machine-readable format
Right to Object: Opt-out of specific processing

To exercise these rights, contact our Data Protection Officer at:

5. Data Security Measures

In compliance with Section 29 (Security Safeguards Principle):
We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures including:

  • End-to-end encryption of all data transmissions
  • Regular security audits by certified professionals
  • Access controls and authentication protocols
  • Pseudonymization of stored data
  • Incident response plan as required by ODPC

In case of a data breach affecting Kenyan citizens, we will notify the ODPC within 72 hours as mandated by law.

6. Third-Party Disclosures

We only share data when legally required:

  • With petition recipients (government bodies) as necessary
  • When ordered by Kenyan courts or regulatory bodies
  • With service providers under strict data processing agreements

Cross-border transfers: Data remains within Kenya unless explicit consent is given, in compliance with Section 48 of the Act.
